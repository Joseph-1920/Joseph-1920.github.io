Five Amazing Astronomical Objects

Read time: 3 minutes

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing can escape, not even light. Black holes are among the strangest, most extreme objects in the Universe.

Neutron stars are incredibly dense astronomical objects that are the remnants of massive stars. As the name suggest, they are largely made of neutrons tightly packed together. Their magnetic fields are so strong that atoms in their influence become distorted.

A star is a luminous spheroid, held together by its own gravity and composed of plasma. They are powered by nuclear fusion. In order of increasing temperature, stars can be red, orange, yellow, white, blue-white, or blue. The Sun is an example of a yellow, main-sequence star near the middle of it's life cycle.

A quasar (quasi-stellar object) is an extremely bright and distant stellar object powered by a supermassive black hole at its centre. Quasars are among the most luminous objects in the universe, outshining entire galaxies, including their own. Research has shown that quasar activity was much more common in the distant past, with peak activity being around 10 billion years ago.

Galaxies are islands of stars, stellar remnants, dust, gas, and dark matter held together by gravity. Most galaxies have a supermassive black hole at their centre. Galaxies can be many different shapes; for example, irregular, elliptical, spiral, barred spiral, or ring.
